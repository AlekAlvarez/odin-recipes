Flan

Description

Flan is a baked custard dessert with a layer of caramel on top. The base is made from eggs, milk or other dairy, sugar, and flavorings, and then poured into a mold or individual baking cups.

The flan is cooked upside down, with the caramel sauce going into the baking dish first, then the custard mixture on top. Flan has a rich, toasted sugar caramel flavor from the topping and the base tastes very milky, perfectly sweet and lightly eggy. It is similar to creme brulee or panna cotta in taste, but texturally the three are quite different.

Ingredients

  • A cup of white sugar is melted on the stove until it's liquified and golden.
  • three whole eggs
  • can of sweetened condensed milk
  • A can of evaporated milk
  • A tablespoon of vanilla

Steps

  1. Melt the sugar on the stove, then pour it into a round baking dish.
  2. Beat the eggs in a large bowl, then stir in the remaining ingredients.
  3. Pour the egg mixture into the baking dish. Cover with foil
  4. Bake for an hour in the preheated oven.
