Tostones

Description

Tostones are a staple dish in Latin American and Caribbean cuisine, and are often served as appetizers or side dishes. They are made by peeling, slicing, cooking, and then smashing green plantains, and then cooking them again. The extra steps give tostones a unique nutty flavor, and they are larger, crunchier, and tastier than regular plantain chips.

Tostones are commonly known as tostones in Jamaica, Puerto Rico, Mexico, Nicaragua, Cuba, U.S.A., Honduras, and Venezuela. In Cuba, they are also known as tachinos or chatinos, and in the Dominican Republic, they are known as platano frito or frito verde.

Ingredients

  • 1 green plantain
  • 5 tablespoons oil for frying
  • 3 cups cold water
  • salt to taste

Steps

  1. Peel plantain and cut into 1-inch slices. Fill a bowl with 3 cups of cold water.
  2. Heat oil in a large deep skillet over medium-high heat; add plantain slices in an even layer and fry on both sides until golden brown, about 3 1/2 minutes per side. Set skillet aside
  3. Transfer plantain slices to a chopping board; flatten each one by placing a small plate on top and pressing down. Dip plantain slices in cold water.
  4. Reheat oil in the skillet over medium heat; cook plantain slices for 1 minute on each side. Season to taste with salt and serve immediately.
